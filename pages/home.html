About Me

Hello, I'm a dedicated computational linguist with a passion for machine translation, breaking down language barriers, and helping rebuild the Tower of Babel, one brick at a time.

I'm currently a PhD student in computer science at Portland State University. A product of an immigrant father from Montenegro and a mother who taught ESL, I grew up in a multilingual household and traveled frequently to Europe. This early exposure to different languages and cultures sparked my lifelong fascination with language and the power of communication.

I was also inspired to study technology after watching Star Wars as a child. My favorite character was C3PO, a protocol droid "fluent in over six million forms of communication" as he liked to boast.

My goal is to help people communicate better, no matter what language they speak. I believe that language is a bridge that connects us all, and I'm committed to building technology that makes communication easier and more accessible for everyone.

And that's computational linguistics. The perfect fusion of computers and language.
